Who are we?

The Maxwell society is the Physics department's student society. Discover the current current committee.

What do we do?

We hold a weekly lecture series with physisists from around thecountry and host a wide variety of social events. We also publish a monthly newsletter to keep you informed of what is going on.

How to join?

All King's students are entitled to be members of the Society, and pay an annual membership fee of £5. Join now!

Events

Regular Maxwell Society events are:

  • The Monday Seminar Series: Seminars on a range of science topics are given by speakers invited from other institutions and also from the King's Physics Department. A list of upcoming events can be found here.
  • The Cumberland Lodge Weekend: This residential weekend takes place near the end of February and is organised around a theme. The theme for 2012 was be The History of Physics at King’s.
  • Social Events: A number of events are planned every year. There is typically a start of year party, an end of year party such as a boat trip and much in between. Suggestions for events are welcome.

Committee

The Maxwell Society Committee consists of a president, secretary, treasurer and several committee members, all chosen from the student body. Elections are held at the end of the academic year.
